Coral Reefs: Guardians of the Ocean

Exploring the beauty and importance of coral reefs in sustaining marine life

🌊 Introduction

Welcome to “The Silent Sea.” This page dives into the magical world of coral reefs and how they serve as the rainforests of the sea. Coral reefs support UN Sustainable Development Goal 14: Life Below Water by sustaining biodiversity, coastal protection, and livelihoods. Yet, they face serious threats from climate change, pollution, and overfishing.

🌐 Importance of Coral Reefs

Coral reefs cover less than 1% of the ocean floor but support 25% of all marine species. Here's how they help our planet and people:

Biodiversity Hotspots

Coral reefs are home to over 1 million species, including fish, mollusks, crustaceans, and many others that rely on this ecosystem to thrive.

Tourism & Recreation

Snorkeling and diving around coral reefs generate billions in global revenue, supporting local economies in tropical regions.

Natural Coastal Defenses

Reefs act as natural breakwaters, reducing wave energy and protecting coastlines from erosion, storms, and floods.

Medicinal Potential

Scientists study coral reef species for medicines to treat cancer, arthritis, and bacterial infections — a hope for future healthcare.

📚 Fascinating Coral Facts

🌍 The Great Barrier Reef

It’s the world’s largest coral reef system, visible from space and home to over 9,000 known species!

⚠️ Coral Bleaching Crisis

Due to warming seas, coral reefs expel the algae they rely on, turning white — a warning sign of stress and potential death.

🐠 Coral Reefs in Sri Lanka

Bar Reef – Kalpitiya

The largest reef in Sri Lanka, Bar Reef supports vibrant marine life but is now threatened by unregulated fishing and coral mining.

Hikkaduwa Coral Sanctuary

Once a thriving tourist destination, this reef faces bleaching and pollution. Conservation efforts are now in place to restore it.
