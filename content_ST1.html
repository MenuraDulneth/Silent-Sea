🌊 Introduction

Welcome to “The Silent Sea.” This section supports UN Sustainable Development Goal 14: Life Below Water. Our oceans regulate climate, provide food, and host diverse marine species – yet they are under severe threat.

⚠️ Major Threats to Marine Life

Overfishing

More than 90% of the world’s fish stocks are overexploited. Overfishing leads to imbalanced ecosystems and declining biodiversity.

Plastic Pollution

11 million tons of plastic enter the ocean yearly, killing wildlife and contaminating food chains.

Ocean Acidification

Rising CO₂ levels are lowering ocean pH, affecting coral reefs and marine organisms with shells.

Climate Change

Warming oceans trigger coral bleaching and habitat loss, severely impacting marine life.

📚 Interesting Ocean Facts

Oceans = Oxygen

Over 50% of Earth’s oxygen is produced by ocean plankton and algae.

Bioluminescence

Many deep-sea creatures glow naturally, a phenomenon called bioluminescence.

🇱🇰 Sri Lanka’s Marine Life

Turtle Conservation

Projects like the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery protect endangered species from extinction.

Coral Restoration

Coral gardens in Hikkaduwa aim to restore reef health damaged by pollution and tourism.

🤝 How You Can Help

Join Cleanups

Participate in local beach cleanups and spread awareness about marine protection.

Eat Responsibly

Choose seafood with sustainability certifications like MSC or ASC to protect fish populations.

Reduce Plastic Use

Switch to reusable bottles, bags, and cutlery to reduce single-use waste.
