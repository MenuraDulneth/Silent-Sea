Ocean and Human Life

Exploring the deep connection between humans and the ocean

🌊 Introduction

Welcome to "The Silent Sea." This section explores the vital connection between oceans and human life while supporting UN Sustainable Development Goal 14: Life Below Water. Oceans regulate our climate, provide food and oxygen, support economies, and nurture rich biodiversity — yet they face increasing threats from pollution, overfishing, and climate change.

🌐 Uses of Oceans for Human Life

Oceans are not only vast bodies of water but essential to life on Earth. This section explores how oceans support our planet and people.

Climate Regulation

Oceans absorb solar heat and distribute it via currents, helping balance global temperatures and stabilize weather patterns. They also absorb CO₂, reducing global warming impacts.

Oxygen Production

Roughly 50% of Earth's oxygen is produced by oceanic phytoplankton and algae, which photosynthesize just like trees on land.

Livelihoods

Fishing, marine tourism, and shipping industries support millions of people worldwide, especially in coastal regions.

Coastal Communities

For many coastal cultures, the ocean is spiritual and central to tradition—from Sri Lankan stilt fishing to Polynesian voyaging.

📚 Interesting Ocean Facts

📏 The Mariana Trench is the Deepest Place on Earth

It's nearly 11,000 meters deep, deeper than Mount Everest is tall!

💙 Millions Depend on the Ocean

About 3 billion people rely on the ocean for food, jobs, or culture.

🐳 Sri Lanka's Ocean and Human Life

Salt Production

In coastal areas like Puttalam and Hambantota, sea water is evaporated under the sun to produce salt—a vital resource for both food and industry in Sri Lanka.

Fishing Communities

Thousands of families in coastal areas like Negombo and Trincomalee depend on the ocean for their livelihood through fishing, trading, and seafood production.
